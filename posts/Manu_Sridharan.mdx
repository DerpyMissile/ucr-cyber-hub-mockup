He is a Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of California, Riverside and a member of the RIPLE research group. 
He works in the areas of programming languages and software engineering, and he is interested in developing tools and techniques to make large-scale software more reliable, performant, secure, and maintainable. 

Previously, he worked on applying PL technologies at Uber. 
He was the primary developer of NullAway, a tool to help eliminate Java null pointer errors with low build-time overhead. 
Before Uber, he worked at IBM Research and at Samsung Research America. 
Even before that, he received my PhD from the University of California, Berkeley.
